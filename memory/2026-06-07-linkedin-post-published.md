## Published Text (User's Final Version)

I'll tell people I use AI and automation to help me with my business and day to day.

I'll hear things like "oh, I don't trust it."

That's when I have to tell them — "Yeah, maybe if you don't have actual systems and structure in place."

Here's what I actually run:

- SOL — orchestrates everything, talks to clients, makes the calls
- CODY — designs architecture before we build anything
- VALI — tests everything, finds bugs I would miss
- PESSI — predicts what will break. Usually right.
- ATLAS — research, docs, remembers what worked
- GENI — visuals, branding, design
- ASSEMBLY — builds and deploys
- CHATTY — communication, outreach
- ORACLE — external consultant (M365 Copilot) when we're stuck
- JURIS — legal & compliance

They're not separate apps or accounts. They're roles inside an OpenClaw system. SOL decides who does what. Every agent returns structured data — not walls of text — so decisions are traceable.

The hard part isn't the AI. It's the orchestration.

This is something I've had to learn over time — not only building my system, but implementing it with others. It's not just about the LLM or even the tools. It's about the structure in which you have your whole system. Making sure the invoice parser hits the right database, the booking system actually sends confirmations, the personal agent doesn't hallucinate client data.

If you're building with AI, the model is maybe 20% of the work. The other 80% is wiring, testing, failing, fixing.
