## Post Text (Facebook Group Version)

Hey everyone! Phillip here — new to the group and excited to connect with other local business owners.

I run Systack, a small automation shop here in Little Rock. We build custom ordering and workflow systems for small businesses that can't afford (or don't want) expensive SaaS subscriptions.

**What that actually means:**

My partner runs The Utopia Deli — amazing food, but like a lot of small food businesses, she was spending 2-3 hours every night on paperwork. Copying orders into Square, confirming catering details by phone, chasing down deposits, answering the same questions over and over.

So we built her a system:

🥪 **Online ordering** — customers browse the full menu, customize their order (extra sauce, no tomato, swap the protein), and checkout through Square. Kitchen gets the order automatically. Zero manual copying.

📋 **Catering/events** — a smart form that captures event details, auto-ranks leads by size/budget/timeline, sends instant email responses with payment terms, and logs everything for follow-up. No more "did they pay the deposit?" confusion.

💰 **The cost:** $0 fixed. Just Square's normal transaction fees.

We built it once. Now we're white-labeling it for other restaurants and food businesses that want the same thing without paying $200+/month for generic platforms.

If you're in food service (or any small business drowning in repetitive busywork), I'd love to chat. Not here to pitch — genuinely curious what friction points you're dealing with.

Drop a comment or DM me. 👇

## Why This Works for Facebook Groups
- **Introduces you first** (Phillip/Systack) — not a faceless company
- **Tells a real story** (partner's deli) — relatable, not theoretical
- **Shows the pain** (2-3 hrs paperwork) — other business owners feel this
- **Shows the solution** — but frames it as "we built this for us" not "buy my thing"
- **Soft CTA** — "curious what friction points you're dealing with" invites conversation
- **No hard links in main text** — Facebook groups often penalize link-heavy posts
- **Links in first comment** — post the links as a comment for better reach

## Posting Strategy
1. Post the main text (no links)
2. Immediately comment with: "Links if you want to check it out: [ordering] [catering] [systack]"
3. Pin that comment
4. Engage with every comment reply
